Rules & Regulations:

  • Round 1: TECH ROUND

    • All teams must do a technical presentation about their respective aircraft. The technical presentation must be minimum 5 minutes and maximum 10 minutes long. The participating model aircraft must be present during the technical presentation. A printed copy of the design report must be submitted to the judges before starting the technical presentation.

  • Round 2: HEAVY LIFTERS

    • A safety inspection will be conducted by the event coordinators before the flight round.  The plane will be loaded with payload(tennis balls). The participant may load as many balls as he deems their aircraft can lift. The plane shall then take off and travel a predefined circuit and drop its payload at the designated payload drop area that will be clearly marked on the ground. Points in this round will be given on the basis of maximum payload lifted and the accuracy of the payload drop.

  • Round 3: FLY HIGH

    • In this round the Pilot shall take the plane to maximum altitude achieved in 20 seconds of upward flight and then cut off the throttle.
    The timer will start once the throttle is cut. The pilot shall then bring the aircraft down and land without the use of throttle.
      The timer will be stopped at the moment the aircraft touches the ground. Maximum points will be given to the aircraft with maximum gliding time.
    RC Aircraft constraints:

    • The model should be a fixed wing UAV only. Lighter-than-air aircrafts are not allowed. Should have only one motor
    • Only electric motor propulsion is allowed. The use of 2.4 GHz radio is required for all aircraft competing in the competition.
    • If the participants want to use any other frequency, they will have to inform the organizers in advance. Motor shaft to propeller RPM ratio must be 1:1.
    • Metal propellers are not allowed.
    • Use of any kind of composite materials or FRPs(fiber reinforced plastics) like carbon fiber or glass fiber is not allowed.
    • Maximum empty weight of the aircraft must be 1300g.
    • Thrust to weight(empty weight) ratio of the aircraft must be less than or equal to 0.8.
    • Wing span of the aircraft must be below 1.2metre.
    • Should use only one 3s or 4s battery lipo battery. All aircraft systems must be powered by a single battery. Aircraft may be hand-launched or may take-off using a landing gear.
    Flight Round Rules:
    • Three attempts will be allowed for take-off in both the flight rounds.
    • Pilots can position themselves at any point on the ground to fly the aircraft during the rounds.
    • During the flight rounds the aircraft must not touch the ground again after initial takeoff or hand-launch before completing the round. In case of contact with the ground, the flight attempt will be disqualified and the aircraft will have to attempt to take-off or launch again.
    • In case of hand-launch the aircraft must be held and released by only one person. Release of aircraft by pilot is prohibited. In case of take-off using landing gear, minimum take-off distance must be 8 metres.

For Glider Design and Flying Competition, a team must design, fabricate, and demonstrate a glider which can perform tasks mentioned in following rounds with given constraint. The Theme of this competition this time is to design and fabricate a glider on-site and compete based on Glide time and Glide Distance and showcase your aerospace and designing skills. The three teams which score the maximum number of points shall win the competition. All the material necessary will be provided to build the gliders. Format of the competition:

  • The event will have 2 rounds, detailed as follows.
    Before proceeding to round 1, each team will be provided with building materials for the glider. And they will be provided 90 minutes for fabricating the glider. Teams are advised to use the materials provided appropriately, as the same glider will be used in the 2nd round and teams can choose to modify the glider from the remaining material from the 1st round.
ROUND 1

The aim of the 1st round for teams is to have the maximum glide time possible for the glider they have fabricated (Launched using the catapult). Depending on glide time, they will be awarded points. All teams from round 1 will then participate in the 2nd round.
Score - Total points = Glide time in (seconds)

ROUND 2

The aim of the 2nd round for teams is to have the maximum gliding distance possible for their glider (Launched using the catapult). Depending on gliding distance, teams will be awarded points. Score - Total points = Glide Distance in (meters) Each Team will be given 3 attempts for both Round 1 and Round 2, best score shall be considered. Depending on the points which teams have scored throughout 2 rounds will be added.
The organizer’s decision shall be final. No queries will be entertained thereafter
